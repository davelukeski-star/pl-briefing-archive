Match of the Week
Man City 2–1 Arsenal
Sunday, April 19 · Etihad Stadium
Goals
Rayan Cherki — City
Kai Havertz 17′ — Arsenal
Erling Haaland 65′ — City

Cherki gave City the lead, Havertz equalized almost immediately, then Haaland did what Haaland does — clinical finish at 65 minutes, game over. Arsenal had the chance to rescue a point in stoppage time: Havertz, free header, six yards out. He put it over the bar. That's the kind of moment that defines a title race.

Saka was absent with his Achilles injury, and it showed. Arsenal looked blunted in the final third every time they needed to create something. City's press was relentless and they deserved the three points.

The standings are now level on 70 points — City ahead only on goals scored (66 vs 63). Five games left. Title momentum has visibly shifted to the Etihad.

Hot Take
Tottenham's relegation crisis isn't Roberto De Zerbi's failure.

It's the predictable result of a decade of transfer-window theatre masquerading as ambition. De Zerbi inherited a squad built for someone else's system, watched his best players leave in the summer, and was given nothing meaningful in January. That's not a manager problem — that's a structure problem.

The board that spent years confusing activity with strategy is the one that should answer for this. Spurs had a Champions League final in 2019 and somehow turned it into a relegation fight by 2026. If they go down, it's the most fitting verdict a front office has ever earned.
