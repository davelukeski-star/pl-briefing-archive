Match of the Week
Saturday, April 11 · Emirates Stadium
Arsenal 1–2 Bournemouth
Arsenal entered Matchweek 32 nine points clear. They left six points clear, without Bukayo Saka (Achilles) or Noni Madueke (knee), beaten by a mid-table side with nothing to play for. Junior Kroupi opened the scoring with a far-post volley from Adrien Truffert's cross. Gyokeres levelled from the spot after Ryan Christie's handball. Then Alex Scott, 74th minute, latched onto Evanilson's cutback and finished. That was that.
The problem was never just about who was missing — it was about what the result exposed. Arsenal have been overperforming their xG by +7.58 all season. Gyokeres' 12 goals are real, but the underlying numbers say this squad does not create enough high-quality chances to coast to a title. Bournemouth came and scored twice. The advantage is real. The comfort is gone.

Race Watch
Title Race
Arsenal sit on 70 points, Man City on 64 with a game in hand. When City play that game, the gap shrinks to three points heading into the Etihad on April 19. Two weeks ago it was nine. One Bournemouth defeat and one City demolition of Chelsea later, this is a two-horse race with one horse galloping faster than the other. April 19 at the Etihad is as close to a title final as the Premier League gets with six games remaining.
UCL Battle
Man United (3rd, 55pts) and Aston Villa (4th, 55pts) are level on points but going in opposite directions. Villa's form reads LLLWD — three straight losses before a win and a draw — and they've scored just 43 goals all season with a UCL spot apparently in the bag. Liverpool (52pts) are three back with six games left and face the Merseyside derby at Everton in MW33. The top four is not closed. Villa need to wake up, fast.
Relegation
Spurs (18th, 30pts), Burnley (19th, 20pts) and Wolves (20th, 17pts) are in the drop zone, but West Ham (17th, 32pts) are just two points clear and Forest (16th, 33pts) only three. Spurs have a new manager and 14 games without a win in the league — De Zerbi's debut loss to Sunderland did nothing to calm anyone. The bottom five is where the real drama lives between now and May.

Hot Take
Arsenal's decision to sign Viktor Gyokeres is the reason they may not win the title — not the reason they will.
He has 12 Premier League goals at MW32, and Arsenal have been overperforming their xG by +7.58 all season — meaning they have been fortunate, not clinical. The loss to Bournemouth, when a mid-table side walked into the Emirates and scored twice, crystallised it: the finishing problem was never fixed. Gyokeres didn't fix it. Six points clear with a trip to the Etihad on April 19 is not comfortable. It is a coin flip.
