Match of the Week
Manchester United 3–2 Liverpool
Old Trafford  ·  Sunday, May 3

Matheus Cunha (6') and Benjamin Sesko (14') had United flying inside 15 minutes. Liverpool clawed back through Szoboszlai (47') and Gakpo (56') to make it level and tense. Then, in the 77th minute, Mac Allister's clearance fell short, Kobbie Mainoo composed himself and guided a low finish into the bottom corner. That was the season right there.

Liverpool were missing both Salah (hamstring) and Isak (groin) — their two most dangerous attackers — which makes the comeback feel somewhat hollow. The better side still won on the night, and that matters. For Michael Carrick's United, going from written-off in October to Champions League qualification is the season's most complete managerial story. They're confirmed in the top four.

For Liverpool, dropping points here with three games left makes the 4th-place race genuinely uncomfortable. They're level on points with Villa but ahead on goal difference — and they face Chelsea this weekend. Chelsea are in freefall, but that's not the same as safe to play.

Hot Take

Forest's explosion is the season's most important and least-discussed story.

Arsenal's title run has absorbed all the attention, but a club that was managerially chaotic and 16th in October is now churning out 14 goals and 2 conceded in five games. That is not survival football — that is a team running hot at exactly the right moment, and Vitor Pereira deserves far more credit than he's getting.

Meanwhile, Chelsea are in a five-game losing streak and playing the FA Cup Final in nine days. At this point their league campaign and their cup run feel like they're happening in parallel universes. One Chelsea is embarrassingly bad. The other is 90 minutes from silverware. Both are real.
