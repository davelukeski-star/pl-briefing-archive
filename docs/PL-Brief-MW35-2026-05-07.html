MATCH OF THE WEEK
Everton 3 – 3 Manchester City
Goodison Park · Monday May 4
A title-race earthquake at Goodison. Jeremy Doku curled City ahead before half-time and the visitors looked comfortable — until 13 minutes of pure chaos turned the title race upside down.
Marc Guehi's loose back-pass let substitute Thierno Barry pounce for the equalizer. Jake O'Brien headed Everton in front. Barry grabbed his second to make it 3-1 and Goodison was levitating. Haaland pulled one back, then Doku — of course Doku — rescued a 97th-minute equalizer that felt less like a comeback and more like a funeral with a polite handshake at the end.
The takeaway: City sit five points behind Arsenal with a game in hand, but the math is academic. The title is now Arsenal's to lose — and if the Gunners refuse to cooperate, Pep won't get a second chance.

Liverpool's loss at Old Trafford was their 18th defeat in all competitions this season. Since their 1962 promotion to the top flight, only three Liverpool sides have lost more in a single campaign — 1992-93 (20), 2004-05 (19), 2009-10 (19). The defending Premier League champions are on pace for their worst defeat tally in over 60 years.
 
HOT TAKE
Slot shouldn't be Liverpool's manager next August.
Full stop — and the fact that the club's hierarchy has publicly backed him is a bigger crisis than any individual result. A defending champion sliding from first to a likely 11th-place finish, conceding more goals than they score, and posting numbers that compare unfavorably to Brendan Rodgers' worst Liverpool side isn't a "bad season" — it's a structural collapse.
The £200m+ Isak/Wirtz duo flopping under Slot's system means he, not the recruitment team, owns this. Loyalty to the man who delivered the title in 2024-25 is admirable; loyalty that allows a 19th-defeat season to be followed by another rebuild under the same coach is how Manchester United lost a decade.
