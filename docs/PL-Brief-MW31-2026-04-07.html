Hot Take

Arne Slot's fatal mistake wasn't tactical — it was that he tried to repurpose Salah as a wide provider in a system that needed Salah to be Salah. By the time that relationship broke down, it took the entire season with it. A defending Premier League champion is now 15 losses deep in all competitions, was humiliated 4-0 by Man City in the FA Cup quarter-final, and fans were singing Xabi Alonso's name leaving the Etihad 10 minutes early. Liverpool didn't need a better Salah this season — they needed a manager who understood that a 33-year-old on his farewell tour needed to be in the box, not stretched to the touchline. Slot's stubbornness on that single tactical question likely cost him the title defence and his job.
